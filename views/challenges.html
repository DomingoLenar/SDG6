Challenges

Access

to clean water and sanitation is a basic human right and essential for sustainable development, yet millions still lack it, particularly in marginalized communities. Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6) aims to ensure universal access by 2030, but challenges like urbanization, pollution, climate change, and poor infrastructure threaten progress. This page highlights these issues and underscores the urgent need for global cooperation, innovation, and sustainable practices to secure water for future generations.

Water Scarcity

1

Many regions globally face water shortages due to factors like overpopulation, over-extraction, climate change, and inefficient water management. Areas in sub-Saharan Africa, the Middle East, and parts of South Asia are particularly affected.

Pollution and Contamination

2

Pollutants from industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and untreated sewage contaminate freshwater sources, threatening ecosystems, human health, and access to safe drinking water.

Access to Sanitation Facilities

3

Globally, around 2 billion people lack access to basic sanitation services, with disparities most notable in rural areas and informal settlements. This leads to health issues like waterborne diseases and restricts overall development.

Regional Disparities

4

Access to water and sanitation varies drastically between regions due to differences in economic status, governance, geographic location, and infrastructure development. For instance, regions in Africa and South Asia lag behind compared to Europe and North America.
